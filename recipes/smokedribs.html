Smoked Ribs

Ingredients

  • Spare Ribs cut and trimmed to preferred style.
  • Rub, marinade, or seasoning of choice.
  • Barbacue sauce, dry spices of choice.

Steps

  1. If marinading the ribs, soak in the marinade of choice for six to twelve hours before cooking in a large cassarole dish with a cover or plastic wrap.
  2. If using a rub, apply rub or seasoning to the ribs on both sides, enough to have good coverage but not so thick that smoke cannot penetrate.
  3. On a smoker, get the heat with the wood of your choice up to 200 degrees fahrenheit. Allow smoker to clear out of white or 'dirty' smoke.
  4. Once the smoker is clear, put the ribs off direct heat bone-side down on the smoker.
  5. Allow ribs to smoke until around 180 degrees fahrenheit as measured at the center of the meat by a thermometer, est. three to four hours.
  6. Once ribs have reached 180, increase the heat of the smoker up to 225-250 degrees fahrenheit. Check temperature regularly.
  7. When the ribs reach 197-201 degrees fahrenheit, remove from the smoker. Wrap in foil or place in a cooler used for the purpose and allow to rest for thirty minutes.
  8. Serve with your choice of barbacue sauce or spices.
