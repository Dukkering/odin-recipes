Sushi

Ingredients

  • 1 cup uncooked short white rice
  • 1 cup cold water
  • 4 rolls of sushi seaweed (nori)
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 5 tbsp mirin or rice vinegar
  • Meat / toppings of your choice (ex. 100g of smoked salmon, 1 cucumber sliced lengthwise, chopped green onion, & cream cheese)
  • Soy sauce, Wasabi, and Pickled Ginger

Steps

  1. Cook rice in water until it boils. Cover and simmer until grains are soft, roughly 30 minutes for 1 cup. Remove from heat and cool.
  2. While rice is cooking, cut your fillings into strips or chop it into pieces as appropriate.
  3. Mix sugar and vinegar or mirin.
  4. Slowly cool the rice without drying it out by turning it by hand while fanning it lightly. Take care not to crush the rice.
  5. Upon the rice being cool enough to touch, mix in the sugar mixture and work it through the rice carefully. When not in use, keep the rice covered with a damp cool cloth.
  6. On your sushi bamboo mat, spread out a piece of nori. For easier clean-up, cover the bamboo mat with plastic wrap first.
  7. Cover the nori with a thin layer of rice, leaving the top 2cm uncovered.
  8. Lay out your choice of fillings horizontally in strips; For an easier time, make a small depression in the line first. Be careful not to overfill,
  9. Using the mat, roll the sushi in on itself firmly up to near the top.
  10. Wet the exposed top of the nori, and roll it up with the rest; The wet nori will glue to the rest of the roll with some pressure.
  11. Using a wet knife, cut the sushi roll into slices of your preferred thickness.
  12. Serve with soy sauce, ginger, and wasabi on the side as preferred.
